Module 9

What will the future of writing look like?

Introduction to Module 9

This week as we return from extended spring break, we’ll regroup & look to the future. First, we’ll explore emerging technologies that challenge our notions of textuality; by that, I mean, what does “writing” mean when the tools & texts change with innovation. What does the future of communication look like if we’re no longer putting pen to page -- or even pressing keys on a keyboard? One day, will we be able to write by thinking? If we’re doing it right, this week’s discussion will go off the rails with creativity and sci-fi-like predictions.

Everything you need to complete for the course is explained and linked below. Start with the Overview to get the gist of what’s required this week.

You can also access text-only Google Document if you prefer.

Instigators are written or multi-modal responses to the readings. What did the reading make you do or think? What's one way to apply the reading? What's your critique or reaction to the reading? Instigators give you a chance to apply and react to the texts that we're reading in this course -- they might also help form our course discussions on Wednesdays.

I'm hesitant to put any parameters on these -- because I want you to dig deep and be creative. Instigators are also a chance to use a medium you don't normally use, to practice creating online content, and to think about the implications of the ideas we're exploring in the course.

My only stipulations:

  • Each instigator response should reflect genuine effort / tie back to the readings in some way; synthesize direct quotations or paraphrases (attribute!!) in your responses.
  • See Course resources for a few examples (I'll be adding to this list)
  • Contain 1-2 questions that you want us to discuss in class
  • Complete 6 of the 12 (3 of which MUST be multimodal responses, i.e., not just a text-based answer)
  • Must be emailed to the professor (laura.price@colostate.edu) by Wednesday at 9am for MWF or Tuesday by 8am for TTh section. Late submissions will not be considered.

Remember that these instigators are worth 18% of your grade; there will be 12 opportunities for instigators -- you must complete 6, and you may complete up to 2 additional for extra credit.

3 of these instigators must use a digital medium other than a simple essay format (meaning you may make a blog post, a podcast, a youtube video, a Photo-essay, a post on social media, a flyer/poster, etc. etc.) Your instigator should also contain 1-2 questions about the readings that you’d like to pose to the class. At the beginning of our Wednesday discussion, we will take a poll on which questions we want to focus on for the discussion.

Each instigator is worth up to 30 pts. Occasionally, bonus points are granted for exceptional execution, creativity, or depth of thought.

Worth: Cumulative and Combined with Workshops

Course work is a broad category that might consist of (1) your in-class writings (prepared and shared in Google Docs at various points) (2) informal group activities (3) Wednesday discussions about readings or (4) Participation in technology workshops on Fridays.

Classwork is cumulative and reflects your engagement with the readings, the discussions & group activities in the course throughout the semester. As long as you are doing the readings and participating in class (either through the in-class writings or discussions/group work), you should do well here. However, factors like consistent absence, disengagement in class, or frequently not reading the assigned readings will lower this grade.

  • A+ (96+) Superior participation
  • A (93-96) Excellent participation
  • A- (90-93) Very good participation
  • B+ ( 86-90) Good participation
  • B (83-86) Acceptable participation
  • B- (80-83) Slight disengagement or lapses in participation
  • C (70-79) Consistent disengagement or frequent missed readings
  • D (60-69) Frequent missed readings, activities and absences
  • F (60 or below) Frequent absences, missed activities, etc.

Worth: Cumulative and Combined with Course Work

For each major assignment, we will conduct at least one peer workshop in class. These workshops give you a chance to critique your peers' work for their benefit as well as yours. To prepare for workshop, you will complete the required amount for the draft, submit the draft on canvas or via email, and attend the in-class portion of the workshop.

Occasionally, I will offer opportunities for extra credit to make up lost points on other assignments; as I do not "round" grades at the end of the semester, keep an eye out for these opportunities that might "bump up" a grade at the end of the semester. Do not rely on extra credit to save a low grade, but extra credit can often (though not always) make a difference when your grade is on the edge of a higher grade.
