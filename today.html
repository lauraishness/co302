Week 3: Identity & Community Online

This week, we'll continue our discussions about networked identities, specifically as it relates to social media. We'll also draft & workshop A1, which is due by 11:59pm on Sunday 2/10 via Canvas or email.

Readings for the week...

  • Excerpts from Jaron Lanier's Who Owns the Future (Read the “Look Inside” preview for the introduction and first chapter; some pages are omitted, but get what you can out of this.)
  • Interact with Blue Feed / Red Feed from the Wall Street Journal (Be sure the review the Methodology section)
  • Watch TED Talk We're building a dystopia just to make people click on ads
  • Watch TED Talk How we need to remake the internet
    • And a few critiques of Lanier's arguments here:
    • Michael Agger's Slate Article
    • Comments archived on a Y-Combinator Forum

Note: For those TED Talks, don't be shy about increasing the play-back speed to 1.5x or even 2x -- both of those speakers talk quite slow and you might enjoy the talk more (and save a bit of time) if you speed it up.

To do...

  • WORKSHOP A1: Draft A1 and be ready to turn in your draft before Friday's peer workshop. Come to class on Friday ready to give feedback to your peers.
  • A1 DUE: Turn in your final draft of A1: Analysis of a Discourse Community by Sunday at 11:59pm on Canvas or via email. Remember that this assignment is worth 12% of your course grade.
