Schedule & Readings: Weeks 1-6

Readings & Instigators due by Wednesdays unless otherwise indicated

Week 1: Introductions

Our first week, we will get familiar with the course materials, syllabus, and begin by defining some key terms & ideas for our course.

Note: normally course readings are due by Wednesday for our discussion class, but this week we're a little off from our normal schedule. Complete these readings before you come to class (see dates listed) and be prepared to discuss.

Readings

  • Friday:Confronting the Myth of the Digital Native
  • Monday of Week 2:Chapter 1 of Douglas Eyman’s “Digital Rhetoric: Theory, Method, Practice"
  • Optional, but helpful if it's been a minute since you've taken a composition/rhetoric course:Backpacks vs. Briefcases
  • Course Syllabus

Week 2: Online Presence / Digital Persona

This week, we'll continue our conversation about ethos & identity online, especially as it relates to online communities & our own identities online. We'll also explore the rhetorical concepts of genre conventions and affordances. These discussions will be crucial for your success in completing our first Major Assignment, A1.

Readings for the week:

  • Consult this Google Doc to see which group you're in for the assigned readings
  • EVERYONE: The Shed at Dulwich (watch)
  • EVERYONE: Affordances
  • GROUP 1: Social Network Sites as Networked Publics: Affordances, Dynamics, and Implications by danah boyd
  • GROUP 2: The Affordances of Social Media Platforms by Taina Bucher and Anne Helmond

Week 3: Identity & Community Online

This week, we'll continue our discussions about networked identities, specifically as it relates to social media. We'll also draft & workshop A1, which is due by 11:59pm on Wednesday 9/18 via Canvas or email.

Workshop A1 -- Friday 2/7

Bring an 80% complete draft (electronic preferred) of A1 to class for review and feedback from your peers.

Readings for the week...

  • Cotter “Playing the visibility game: How digital influencers and algorithms negotiate influence on Instagram” from New Media & Society [Proprietary PDF on Canvas]
  • Excerpts from Jaron Lanier's Who Owns the Future (Read the “Look Inside” preview for the introduction and first chapter; some pages are omitted, but get what you can out of this.)
  • Interact with Blue Feed / Red Feed from the Wall Street Journal (Be sure the review the Methodology section)
  • Watch TED Talk We're building a dystopia just to make people click on ads
  • Watch TED Talk How we need to remake the internet
    • (OPTIONAL) And a few critiques of Lanier's arguments here:
    • Michael Agger's Slate Article
    • Comments archived on a Y-Combinator Forum

Note: For those TED Talks, don't be shy about increasing the play-back speed to 1.5x or even 2x -- both of those speakers talk quite slow and you might enjoy the talk more (and save a bit of time) if you speed it up.

Week 4

Continue polishing A1; consider swinging by office hours (Eddy 325, Mondays & Wednesdays from 2-3:30pm and by appt) or the CSU Writing Center as well.; We'll also begin our second unit on web design.

Note that Assignment 1: Analysis of a discourse community is due by 11:59pm on Wednesday 2/12. Submit on Canvas (or via email if you encounter issues submitting through Canvas)

Readings for the week...

  • d.School “An Introduction to Design Thinking Process Guide” by the Institute of Design at Stanford
  • Norman’s “Interface Copy Impacts Decision Making”
  • W3.org’s “Tips for Getting Started Writing for Web Accessibility”

Week 5

This week we'll dive into markup language -- both some theorizing around it as well as the practice of composing online. Note that if you are already quite proficient in HTML/CSS, you may be asked to assist your peers in also learning these literacies.

Readings for the week...

  • Haas’s “Wampum as Hypertext” [Proprietary PDF on Canvas]
  • Ong “Writing is a Technology that Changes Thought” [Proprietary PDF on Canvas]
  • “What is the Internet?” Watch the whole series. (8 short videos)
  • Exercises on Codecademy (set up a free account); work through several of the exercises on their Introduction to HTML series.

Week 6

This week we'll continue our coding practice while also exploring issues surrounding copyright & intellectual property.

Readings for the week...

  • Lessig's TED Talk: “Laws that Choke Creativity”
  • Skim and read one section of Amidon et. al. Copyright, Content, and Control: Choose one section of the "Platforms" section (either Turnitin, Twitter, or Canvas) to explore in more details.
  • No additional readings; continue working towards A2
